Who I Am

I am currently completing dual degrees in Technology & Information Management and Business Management Economics at UC Santa Cruz. My academic interests lie in understanding how economic systems interact with technological change, institutional decision-making, and global operational networks.

In my senior Management of Technology capstone, I am leading product strategy and commercialization planning for a combined washer-dryer system developed in collaboration with Whirlpool Corporation. This experience has required integrating economic cost analysis, supply chain constraints, and consumer demand considerations within real-world design tradeoffs.

Beyond academics, I founded and operate Y2Slay, a resale venture focused on luxury fashion and consumer electronics markets. Managing pricing strategies, procurement decisions, and logistics in volatile resale environments has reinforced my interest in market behavior, information asymmetry, and global trade dynamics.

My professional experience in technical roles at hardware firms has further shaped my perspective on how economic incentives, operational systems, and organizational structures influence firm performance.

I am particularly interested in economic policy, global service industries, and institutional responses to market disruptions. In the future, I intend to pursue graduate study and careers focused on strategic operations, economic analysis, and technology-driven industries.
