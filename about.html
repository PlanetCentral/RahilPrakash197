Who I Am

I currently am pursuing dual degrees in both Technology & Information Management (B.S.) and Business Management Economics (B.A.) at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My academic interests mainly focus on how technology, economic systems, and organizational decision making are able to intersect in order to shape consumer experiences and global markets.

Whilst much of my formal experience has been within technical and analytical coursework, my broader perspective has been by longstanding personal interests in global service industries; including finance, travel, aviation, and luxury retail. From a rather young age, I was fascinated and excited by how companies such as American Express have designed integrated, financial & travel ecosystems, alongside how retailers such as Neiman Marcus have built brand value via both operational strategy, and consumer experiences. These interests have greatly influenced how I think about economic coordination at a larger scale— not as just theoretical models, but as systems which shape consumer behavior and organizational resilience.

Academically, I've focused on translating technical decisions into economic and strategic outcomes. In my senior Management of Technology capstone, I'm coordinating to lead product strategy and commercialization for a combined washer-dryer system developed for the Whirlpool Corporation. This project necessitates balancing cost modeling, supply chain feasibility, and market positioning under real world constraints.

Outside of my academic work, I founded and operate Y2Slay— a resale venture focused upon luxury fashion and high-grade consumer electronics. I manage procurement, pricing, and logistics in rather volatile resale environments. This has strengthened my interests especially within market dynamics and global trade systems. My professional experience as well within technical roles at hardware firms— including as a Project Coordinator at Aeva Inc, and Electronic Technician at Cutera Inc, have further reinforced my understandings in how organizational efficiency and economic incentives directly influence technological execution.

Broadly, I'm interested in how firms are able to design systems which remain resilient amid major economic uncertainties, and evolving consumer expectations. I aspire to continue exploring how economic analysis informs strategy, operations, and policy decisions throughout global industries.
