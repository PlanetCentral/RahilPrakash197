Presentation — Strategic Approaches to Fiscal Equilibrium

Presentation Overview

This presentation accompanies a paper examining sustainable balanced budgets as a structural commitment to long-term fiscal health. It argues that fiscal discipline functions as a pillar of national saving, a mechanism for lowering interest rates across the economy, and a foundation for preserving the U.S. dollar's status as the global reserve currency.

Core Arguments

  • Cyclically adjusted balance as a framework for fiscal equilibrium, allowing automatic stabilizers to operate without compromising long-run sustainability
  • The crowding-out mechanism: how persistent deficits reduce national saving, raise the cost of capital, and displace private investment in productive capacity
  • Interest rate effects on American families, from mortgage costs and auto loans to credit card debt and student loan repayments
  • The credibility premium: how multi-year fiscal accountability lowers Treasury bond risk premiums and reduces borrowing costs economy-wide

International Models

  • Switzerland's constitutional debt brake, which reduced debt-to-GDP from 56% to approximately 33% by linking spending to cyclically adjusted revenue
  • Germany's structural deficit rule limiting the federal government to 0.35% of GDP, establishing fiscal anchoring across the Eurozone
  • The UC Regents' Debt Affordability Model as a domestic institutional example of binding fiscal constraints within a large public system

Policy Proposals

  • A Balanced Budget Amendment with supermajority waiver requirements to create durable, constitutional fiscal discipline
  • Constraining government spending growth to the rate of population growth plus inflation as a transparent, sustainable budgeting measure
  • Preserving the dollar's reserve currency status against the backdrop of declining global share (from 70% in 1999 to 58% in 2024) and rising debt-to-GDP ratios
