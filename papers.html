Paper 1: Hidden Travel & Economic Behavior

This paper analyzes the economic implications of "hidden travel" practices within airline pricing systems. It examines how consumer responses to pricing inefficiencies influence airline revenue models, route planning, and regulatory considerations.

Key Themes

  • Price discrimination and market segmentation in airline fare structures
  • Consumer arbitrage behavior in response to complex pricing models
  • Airline cost structures and revenue optimization strategies
  • Policy and ethical implications of opaque fare design

The paper explores how technological transparency and pricing complexity shape both consumer incentives and firm strategy in global aviation markets. As travelers gain access to more sophisticated booking tools, the dynamics between carriers and consumers continue to evolve, raising important questions about efficiency, fairness, and regulatory oversight.

Paper 2: Public Safety Policy & Economic Outcomes

This paper evaluates the economic consequences associated with policing policy changes in San Francisco, focusing on business activity, public investment confidence, and recovery strategies.

Areas of Analysis

  • Urban economic stability and public safety externalities
  • Local business performance and investment signals
  • Fiscal policy responses and municipal budgeting pressures
  • Recovery pathways through institutional coordination

The paper emphasizes the relationship between governance decisions and economic resilience in metropolitan environments. By examining San Francisco's experience, it draws broader lessons about how public safety infrastructure interacts with business confidence, property markets, and long-term urban economic health.
